<h4>Tools for the Journey</h4>
<p>The 50% Journey is built on the idea that rewriting your future is a skill — and like any skill, it develops through practice. These resources support the four-step framework from the book.</p>

<br/>
<h5>Goal Setting Methods</h5>
<p>The book outlines seven approaches to finding your next 90-day goal. Use whichever fits your starting point:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nexus Method</strong> — Find where your Competence, Passion, and Contribution overlap</li>
<li><strong>Begin From the End</strong> — Work backward from press releases, acceptance speeches, or your obituary</li>
<li><strong>Survey Method</strong> — Tap coworkers, friends, and family for what they see in you</li>
<li><strong>Reverse Engineering</strong> — Trace your best days, ideal future, and most fulfilled moments</li>
<li><strong>Storyline Method</strong> — Write your life as memoir, science fiction, or from your antagonist's view</li>
<li><strong>The "Why Not?" Method</strong> — Revisit dismissed dreams and examine the barriers honestly</li>
<li><strong>Experimental Method</strong> — Run a 90-day trial, try the opposite of your default, or commit to one thing fully</li>
</ul>

<br/>
<h5>Guardrail Setting Methods</h5>
<p>Goals without guardrails get abandoned. These methods help you identify the values that need protecting during your sprint:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>One Word Method</strong> — Use Five Why's, Non-Negotiables, or Reductive stripping to find your core value</li>
<li><strong>Aspiration Method</strong> — Study your mentors, heroes, and anti-heroes for what you admire and reject</li>
<li><strong>Begin From the End</strong> — Write a legacy letter, choose three tombstone words, or get advice from your future self</li>
<li><strong>Self Connection Method</strong> — Get present through breath, name your feelings, trace them to unmet needs</li>
<li><strong>Elimination Method</strong> — Sacrifice, Live Without, and Universality to test what's genuinely essential</li>
</ul>
